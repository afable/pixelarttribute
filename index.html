Pixel Art — a tribute



A spec of history

  • Pixel art is a form of art that gets its beginnings from the raster graphics era of the 1980's.
  • Images were edited by the pixel thus constructing an art piece defined by square blocks.
  • Pixel art designs are much like an embroidery or mosaic work that are closely tied to rigid patterns.
  • Adele Goldberg and Robert Flegal of Xerox Palo Alto Research Center published the term pixel art in the Communications of the ACM Magazine in 1982.
  • Pixel art garnered popularity due to its use in video games since the 1980's.
  • Marmoset created a grid-based painting tool called Hexels that creates polygon art similar to pixel art.
